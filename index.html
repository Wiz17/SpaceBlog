Event Horizon Telescope

The first photo of a black hole was captured by the Event Horizon Telescope (EHT) project, an international collaboration of scientists. The image, which was unveiled on April 10, 2019, was of the supermassive black hole located at the center of the Messier 87 (M87) galaxy.Although the photo was a collective effort, it is important to note that no single person took the photo. Instead, the image was produced by combining data collected from a network of radio telescopes around the world using a technique called very-long-baseline interferometry (VLBI)

Hubblespace Telescope

The Hubble Space Telescope has made numerous groundbreaking scientific discoveries across various fields of astronomy. It has provided crucial insights into the age of the universe, the expansion rate of the universe (Hubble's Law), the existence of dark matter and dark energy, the formation of galaxies, the lifecycle of stars, and the identification of exoplanets, among others. Hubble is renowned for capturing breathtaking images of celestial objects. Its high-resolution images have provided stunning views of distant galaxies, nebulae, star clusters, and supernovae. These images have not only expanded our knowledge of the cosmos but have also sparked public interest and appreciation for the beauty of the universe.

International Space Station

The ISS is a joint project involving the space agencies of multiple nations. The major partners include NASA (United States), Roscosmos (Russia), JAXA (Japan), ESA (European Space Agency), and CSA (Canadian Space Agency). These agencies work together to build, maintain, and operate the space station. The ISS serves as a home and workplace for astronauts and cosmonauts from different countries. The crew typically consists of a mix of astronauts from various partner nations. Crews usually stay on the station for several months, conducting scientific experiments, performing maintenance, and supporting ongoing research.
